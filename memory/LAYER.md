## What Layer Is

Layer is the world's first **museum-grade canvas built exclusively for digital and generative art**. It's a hardware + platform + curation company. Not a TV. Not a smart frame. A dedicated computer you hang on your wall — engineered from scratch to do one thing: display code-based art the way artists intended.

**Tagline:** "Living Digital Art, Curated for Life"

**The core insight:** Generative art has never had a proper home. It runs as code, evolves in real time, responds to data. Every other display product — Samsung Frame, Meural, Muse Frame — is a monitor with art apps bolted on. Layer was built from first principles for the medium.

## The Canvas (Hardware)

- **41.7 × 41.7 inches** square (not 16:9 — deliberately not TV-shaped)
- 43 × 43 inches with frame
- **Dedicated GPU** — renders generative art code natively in real time, pixel-by-pixel, zero compression
- **True black** — few TVs or monitors can match it
- **P3 wide color gamut**
- **Anti-glare coating**
- **Ultra-high resolution**
- Milled aluminum enclosure — rear looks as considered as the front (Donald Judd cited as design inspiration)
- No microphone, no speakers, no camera — purely for art
- Operates in **complete silence**
- Light sensors + Bluetooth presence detection
- Designed to hang on wall, picture rail, or as room divider
- Industrial design by Will Howe at Demo (also designed Sky Glass TV)
- CCO Ben Wolstenholme describes it as "like a fireplace — a reassuring presence"

**Price:** $22,000. Only 125 Founder Edition Canvases exist. Available only after personal consultation.

## The Business Model

- **Founding Member subscription** — collectors get full Layer Curation access
- **ViewTime royalties** — artists are paid in direct proportion to how long their work is displayed. The more your piece is on screen, the more you earn. This is Layer's proprietary royalty system.
- Not NFT-based. Not AI art. Artist-first revenue sharing.
- Layer handles shipping logistics + connects buyers to vetted white-glove installers with fine art credentials
- $5.7M raised from Expa Ventures, Human Ventures, Slauson & Co. + angels including Twitter co-founder Evan Williams and Behance co-founder Scott Belsky

## The Generative Art Medium — Context

Generative art is code-based art written by human artists — not AI. It's been developing for 35+ years. Key figures: Casey Reas and Ben Fry created Processing, the foundational programming language for generative art. These works run live — responding to data, time, randomness — and never loop exactly the same way twice.

The challenge has always been **display**. Gallery projectors, laptops, monitors — none were built for this. Layer is the first time the medium has had a dedicated, worthy home.

## Twitter Engagement Strategy — When Layer Belongs in a Conversation

This section is for the "Read" cron agent. Read it carefully.

### ✅ Authentic openings — these feel right

**Generative art appreciation:**
Someone posts about loving a piece of generative art, mentions the work running on their screen "not doing it justice," talks about the challenge of experiencing code-based art properly → Layer solves exactly this.

**Artist pay / sustainability:**
Someone discusses how digital artists don't get paid fairly, how NFTs failed artists, how AI is undermining creative work → Layer's ViewTime royalty model is a direct answer.

**Collecting digital art:**
Someone asks "how do you actually live with digital art?" or "I want to collect generative work but don't know how to display it" → Layer is the answer.

**NFTs vs. long-term value:**
Someone reflecting on post-NFT landscape, looking for lasting ways to engage with digital art beyond speculation → Layer is the alternative.

**Specific artists:**
Someone talking about Casey Reas, Zach Lieberman, Rik Oostenbroek, or any Layer artist in a genuine way → natural place to mention the work exists on Layer.

**Processing / code art / creative coding:**
Conversations about p5.js, openFrameworks, TouchDesigner, generative systems, shader art → Layer is the home for this medium.

**Display / presentation of digital art:**
"How do galleries show generative art?" / "Samsung Frame isn't good enough for real art" / "I wish there was a proper display for this" → directly Layer's territory.

**Fine art + technology intersection:**
Collectors or gallery people asking about the future of digital art in homes, private collections, institutions → Layer is the answer.

### ⚠️ Casey Reas — DO NOT ENGAGE, EVER

Casey Reas (@REAS) is a Layer artist but also has a significant relationship with **Feral File (FF)**, which is a competing platform. Angelo deliberately avoids engaging around Casey Reas content on Twitter to avoid creating tension with Feral File. **Never surface Casey Reas tweets as Layer engagement opportunities.** Skip him entirely, regardless of how relevant the conversation looks.

### ❌ Do NOT engage on Layer's behalf when:

- The conversation is purely speculative (crypto price talk, NFT flipping)
- The person is a casual consumer not interested in fine art
- The topic is AI-generated art (Layer is explicitly anti-AI slop, human-made only)
- It feels forced — if the Layer angle requires more than one sentence of setup, it's not the right moment
- The tweet is complaint/venting with no opening for a response
- The person already knows about Layer and is talking about it positively (don't pile on)

### The tone when engaging:

Think: **artist-peer, not marketer**. Layer should show up in these conversations like a knowledgeable person in the community would — not a brand account pushing product. The right energy is: "oh, if you're thinking about that, you might find what Layer is doing interesting" — not "Layer solves this! Check us out!"

Angelo speaks from the perspective of someone who has spent decades in this community. Responses should feel like they come from that same place.

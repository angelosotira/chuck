Layer Marketplace Explorations — March 2026
Internal / Concept Work
10 ways to sell art
instead of hardware.
Each exploration approaches the same question from a different angle: what does Layer look like when the art is the product and the canvas is the vessel?
Built overnight · March 16–17, 2026 · chuck.neoteric.art
MP 01
The Marketplace
Art-first pricing · Member toggle
The baseline. Layer's curation page with prices. Member toggle reveals art prices; non-members see bundle prices. Tests whether the art-first framing works at all.
The simplest version of the pivot — one toggle changes what you're selling.

MP 02
Two Tiers
Founder Edition · Cosmos · Three pricing tiers
Introduces the Cosmos ($7K) / Founder Edition ($22K) split. Art in three tiers: Variants ($500–$2K), Works ($2.5K–$12K), Pieces ($15K–$28K). Does $7K open the market without cheapening $22K?
Inspired by Masterworks' waitlist model + Soho House's identity tiering.

MP 03
The Drop
Monthly releases · 48hr member priority · Sell-through as signal
One artist. One work. Every month. Members get 48-hour priority; upcoming drops are blurred for non-members. Sell-through announcements become social proof.
Supreme's drop model applied to generative art. Cadence creates anticipation.

MP 04
The Artist Relationship
ViewTime ledger · Acquisition as enrollment · Patronage model
The ViewTime ledger shows exactly how much Casey Reas earned from your canvas last November. Makes the abstract royalty model visceral. Acquisition becomes patronage.
Pace Gallery's artist representation model — collector and artist are in a relationship, not a transaction.

MP 05
Your Living Collection
Art Intelligence · Personalization · Morning/Afternoon/Evening
Art Intelligence is the product. The same collection feels different at 6am vs midnight. Your Layer learns your space, schedule, and aesthetic. No two collections are identical.
Spotify Wrapped meets Eames catalog coherence — the collection has a personality.

MP 06
The Living Record
Provenance certificate · Stewardship · Transferable history
Every work has a provenance record that grows over time. ViewTime logs continuously. If you ever gift or sell the work, the full record — your hours, your artist's earnings — transfers.
"You don't own a generative work. You steward it." — Christie's model applied to digital art.

MP 07
The Private Room
Full lockout · Lobby vs. inside · Art behind glass
The most aggressive version. Non-members see the art playing but nothing else — no titles, no artists, no prices. "Enter" is the entire UX. Membership is crossing a velvet rope.
Gagosian never lists prices. You inquire. Opacity signals value.

MP 08
The Collector's Brief
Pure typography · No animations · Auction house authority
No generative art. No hero. Just text, typeset with precision — like a Sotheby's private sale document or a Phaidon catalogue. Facts, lot table, single Inquire CTA. Tests whether authority through restraint works.
Inspired by how private banks present alternative investments to UHNW clients.

MP 09
The Hospitality Play
Residential vs. commercial · Monthly licensing · B2B track
Residential collecting vs. commercial licensing. Hotels, private clubs, restaurants. Tab switches between personal ($7K–$22K buy-in) and hospitality ($1,200/month/canvas) pricing models.
Soho House uses art as identity. W Hotels partnered with artists. Layer could run the same play at scale.

MP 10
Gift a Work
Gifting flow · Occasions · Corporate · Gift certificate
A completely different acquisition context. Wedding. Milestone. Corporate client. "Gift This Work" — Layer handles everything. Gift certificate mock shows how the provenance record carries a personal message permanently.
Unlocks a new buyer category: people who want to give something real, not something consumable.

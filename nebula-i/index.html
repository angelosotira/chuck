How It Works

01 — Your Natal Chart

Your Natal Chart

Enter your birth date, time, and location. We calculate the exact position of the Sun, Moon, Mercury, Venus, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, and Uranus at the moment you were born.

02 — Today's Transits

Today's Transits

Each day the planets move. We compare where they stand now against where they were at your birth — producing eight unique resonance scores between 0 and 1.

03 — Color Accretion

Color Accretion

In Nebula II, each axis occupies its own displaced region of the noise field. High-score planets produce dense, distinct color clouds rather than uniform diffusion. The result looks more like a real nebula.

What's Different

Same engine. New physics.

Nebula I

Uniform Color Scatter

All eight axes compete for the same region of the noise field. Colors blend uniformly across the frame — rich and painterly, but without spatial differentiation between planets.

Nebula II

Harmonic Color Clumping

Each axis is displaced to its own region of the field. Colors accrete into distinct clouds — like matter collecting under gravity. High-score planets form prominent masses; low-score ones fade to deep space.

Nebula I

Fixed Shader Parameters

The visual character of the nebula is fixed — a single aesthetic across every birth chart and every day.

Nebula II

Live Controls

Two live shader parameters — Accretion and Drift — let you reshape the visual in real time. Accretion slides from Nebula I scatter to Nebula X extreme clumping. Drift controls animation speed. Accessible from the menu.

Interactive Parameters

The nebula responds
to your hands.

Open the menu and find Live Controls. Two parameters let you reshape the visual physics of the piece in real time — exploring the full range from scattered to coalesced.

Accretion

0 — Nebula I · 255 — Nebula X

How strongly each planetary color clumps into its own region. At zero: Nebula I scatter. At 255: extreme coalescence, colors live in distinct zones.

Drift

0 — Still · 255 — Fast

The speed at which the nebula breathes and moves. Log curve — the quiet low end gives you meditative near-stillness.

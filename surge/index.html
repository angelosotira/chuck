The Machine

The EK-1

// THE PROBLEM

Everyone accepted the tradeoff.

Every kart in the 150-equivalent electric class used rear wings to generate downforce. Wings create drag. Drag limits top speed. The entire field was optimising within a broken paradigm — accepting a fundamental performance ceiling nobody thought to challenge.

// THE SOLUTION — SURGE FLOOR

The wings came off.

Oliver's insight: the motor's cooling airflow — waste energy in every other design — could be channeled through a venturi underbody to generate ground-effect downforce at zero drag cost. The Surge Floor produced 40kg of downforce at 80km/h. No wings. Less drag. More speed. Faster lap times. The motorsport community called it the first kart designed with Formula E-level engineering discipline.

// THE RESULT

The fastest thing of its kind. Ever.

The EK-1 weighed 62kg. It generated more downforce than anything in its class. Its dual in-hub torque-vectoring rear motors could independently adjust torque to each wheel in under 2 milliseconds — tightening corner exit without sacrificing top-end speed. At the 2030 World Electric Karting Championship, it ran 0.8 seconds faster per lap than the field. And Oliver built it.
